New to Dota?

Dota 2 is a highly competitive multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game where two teams, the Radiant and the Dire, clash in a strategic battle for supremacy. Each player controls a powerful hero with unique abilities, working together to destroy the enemy's Ancient while defending their own. The game is renowned for its depth, teamwork, and dynamic gameplay.

One of Dota 2's standout features is its diverse roster of over 100 heroes, each with its own distinct set of abilities and playstyle. From agile assassins to towering tanks, players can choose heroes that suit their preferred role and playstyle, adding layers of complexity and variety to every match.

Dota 2 is celebrated for its strategic depth, requiring players to make critical decisions throughout the game. From drafting the right heroes during the pick phase to executing well-coordinated teamfights and split-second decisions during intense skirmishes, strategic thinking is paramount to success.

Dota 2 Origins

The origin of Dota 2 can be traced back to a custom map for the game Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos and its expansion, Warcraft III: The Frozen Throne. DotA was created by a mapmaker named Eul in the early 2000s. The map gained popularity, and its development continued under the contributions of various modders and the gaming community.

One of the prominent versions of DotA was developed by a modder known as IceFrog, who played a significant role in shaping the game and introducing new features. DotA Allstars, a variant of the original DotA, became particularly popular and attracted a large player base.

Valve Corporation, recognizing the popularity of DotA, decided to work with IceFrog to create a standalone sequel. This collaboration led to the development and release of Dota 2, which was officially launched by Valve on July 9, 2013.

Dota 2 retained many of the core mechanics and heroes from the original DotA while introducing updated graphics, new heroes, and additional features. The transition to a standalone game on the Source engine allowed Dota 2 to become a major player in the Esports scene and one of the most popular MOBA games globally.

Game Objectives

The primary objective in Dota 2 is to destroy the enemy Ancient, a heavily guarded structure located within each team's base. To achieve this, players must navigate through three lanes, defeat enemy creeps, and strategically push into the opponent's territory. Along the way, securing objectives like towers and Roshan, the powerful neutral monster, provides significant advantages.

Dota 2's map is divided into three lanes: top, middle, and bottom. Each lane is guarded by defensive structures called towers, which must be destroyed to advance further into enemy territory. In addition to pushing lanes, players can also farm neutral creeps in the jungle, a neutral territory between lanes, to gain gold and experience, further enhancing their hero's power.

Securing Roshan, a formidable neutral monster located in the center of the map, can turn the tide of the game. Killing Roshan grants the team who slays it the Aegis of the Immortal, a powerful resurrection item that allows a hero to respawn upon death. Additionally, Roshan drops the Cheese, Refresher Shard, and Aghanim's Blessing, valuable items that provide significant advantages in teamfights.

As players progress through the game, they must carefully manage their resources, including gold and experience, to purchase items and level up their heroes. Itemization plays a crucial role in Dota 2, as the right combination of items can enhance a hero's strengths, mitigate weaknesses, and synergize with their abilities and playstyle.

Team coordination and strategic decision-making are essential for success in Dota 2. Players must communicate effectively, coordinate ganks, and execute well-timed teamfights to gain map control, secure objectives, and ultimately destroy the enemy Ancient.

Game Preparation

Before diving into the main game, players must select a hero from a diverse pool, each with unique strengths and abilities. The drafting phase, where heroes are chosen and banned, is crucial for creating a balanced and synergistic team composition. Proper communication and planning during this phase can set the tone for success in the upcoming match.

Main Game

The main game unfolds in distinct phases—early, mid, and late game—each requiring different strategies. Players must focus on farming gold and experience, purchasing items to enhance their hero's capabilities, and coordinating with teammates to secure objectives. Teamfights, ganks, and map control become pivotal as the battle intensifies, with the ultimate goal of breaching the enemy base and destroying the Ancient.

Dota 2 in Esports

Dota 2 is one of the leading titles in Esports and competitive gaming. It boasts a massive player base and a vibrant professional scene with tournaments, leagues, and championships held worldwide.
Here are some key aspects of Dota 2's presence in Esports:

International Tournaments

• The pinnacle of Dota 2 Esports is "The International" (TI), an annual tournament organized by Valve Corporation. TI features the best teams from around the world competing for a multi-million dollar prize pool. It is renowned for its large-scale production, intense competition, and passionate fanbase.


Courtesy of Statista

Professional Leagues

• In addition to TI, Dota 2 has numerous professional leagues and circuits hosted by organizations like ESL, DreamHack, and Beyond The Summit. These leagues offer regular competitions, giving teams opportunities to earn prize money, qualify for major tournaments, and showcase their skills.


Regional Competitions

• Dota 2's Esports ecosystem includes regional competitions in regions like North America, Europe, China, Southeast Asia, and CIS (Commonwealth of Independent States). These regional tournaments serve as qualifiers for larger events and help foster talent development within specific regions.


Teams and Players

• Professional Dota 2 teams, often sponsored by organizations or backed by investors, compete in various tournaments and leagues. Players, revered for their skill and expertise, form the backbone of these teams and are highly valued for their abilities in the game.


Streaming and Content Creation

• Many Dota 2 players, both professional and casual, engage in streaming and content creation on platforms like Twitch and YouTube. Streaming provides an additional source of income for players and allows fans to watch their favorite players in action, learn from their gameplay, and interact with them in real-time.


Community Engagement

• Dota 2 has a passionate and engaged community that actively participates in discussions, creates fan content, and supports the Esports scene. Community-driven initiatives, such as amateur tournaments, fan-run events, and online forums, contribute to the overall growth and sustainability of the game's competitive ecosystem.
